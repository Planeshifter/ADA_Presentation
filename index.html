Using an ontology: WordNet

  • Lexical Database developed by George Miller and colleagues at Princeton University over last two decades
  • Organized around the notion of a synset: set of synonyms which represents a single concept.
  • Contains 117 000 synsets which are linked to each other by a small number of “conceptual relations”

Fellbaum, Christiane (2005). WordNet and wordnets. In: Brown, Keith et al. (eds.), Encyclopedia of Language and Linguistics, Second Edition, Oxford: Elsevier, 665-670

What is a synset, again?

{dollar, buck, clam}

WordNet’s Lexical Hierarchy

Contains hierarchy of concepts, ranging from very general ({entity}, {state}) to moderately abstract ({person, individual}) to very specific ({doctor, doc, physician}).

Some terminology: For a given synset, its. . .

  • hypernyms are the synsets that are more general
  • hyponyms are the synsets that are more specific

Hyponyms have an “Is-A" relationship to their hypernyms.

Synset Co-Occurence

Let

  • $X_i = \{0,1\}$ indicator of observing synset $X$ in doc $i$
  • $Y_i = \{0,1\}$ indicator of observing synset $Y$ in doc $i$

Pearson Correlation / Phi Coefficient:

  • $\rho_{X,Y} = \frac{\mathbb{E}\left[ XY \right] - \mathbb{E}\left[ X \right] \mathbb{E}\left[Y\right]}{\sqrt{\mathbb{V}\left[ X \right] \mathbb{V}\left[ Y \right]} } \implies \hat \rho_{X,Y} = \frac{n_{11} n_{00} - n_{10} n_{01}}{\sqrt{n_{1\cdot}n_{0\cdot}n_{\cdot1}n_{\cdot 0}}} $
